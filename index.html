Programming is like Cooking üç≥


The coding environment is very much like a kitchen. So let's ask, how is your experience in the kitchen? Is your kitchen organized? When you cook, do you follow any sort of plan or methodology? During or after cooking, does the kitchen look like an action scene from a movie directed by Michael Bay? Would Gordon Ramsay approve?


Just like recipes in cooking, code files are the sets of instructions that you give the computer to "run" or to carry out. Generally speaking, these instructions are carried out one line at a time, from top to bottom. The computer will do EXACTLY what you tell it to do in the code. This is why your entire program can fail completely due to a simple error like missing a closing > or ). This is also why we sometimes refer to computers as being "stupid" or "naive". Because of this circumstance, as we write our code we should be as elegant and as organized as possible.


Consider the incredibly arduous process of making a cup of instant noodles:


Cup Noodles

Instructions:

  • 1. Boil water
  • 2. Pour boiling water into noodle cup
  • 3. Wait until it's cooked
  • 3. Eat?

As humans, most of us (I hope) can easily follow these directions. Now imagine if there was a robot that tried to follow these directions EXACTLY. You may be asking yourself:

  • What source of water should the robot use?
  • How method will it use to boil the water? A stove-top kettle?
  • How much water should it boil?
  • Did it know to OPEN the lid to the cup before pouring the water into it? If yes, how much water should it pour in?
  • How long should it wait for it be cooked?
  • How will it eat?

Now imagine how many lines of code (or steps of logic) that a massively complex program must "run". There are countless ways that it could break! I cannot stress enough the importance of being organized!

Anyway, the takeaway from all of this is: the better your approach to programming is, the better your experience will be.
